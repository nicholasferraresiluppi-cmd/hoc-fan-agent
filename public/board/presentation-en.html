02 — THE GOAL
What the leaderboard is for

Raise the average score
of the whole agency, month after month.

A higher average agency score means more productive chatters, higher total sales, better margins. The leaderboard isn't just a ranking: it's the starting point to decide where to intervene.

Average agency score
→ 60

We work to shift the median, both by raising the level of middle performers and by removing chronic underperformers from the tail.

How the average rises
  • ↑ Middle performers grow thanks to targeted training based on the gaps the leaderboard reveals
  • ✕ Chronic underperformers exit and are replaced by those who already perform well on those creators
  • ★ Top performers get recognized and become a reference for new hires (mentor / case study)
03 — THE LEVERS
The 2 operational mechanisms

Two levers, one direction:
higher average score.

HOC Pro isn't just a ranking. From every listed operator, concrete decisions follow: grow them or replace them. The leaderboard is the input, these 2 levers are the output.

1

Targeted training

For those who are in and have room to grow. The leaderboard shows the exact gap: good at chatting but converts poorly? coaching on closing / PPV strategy. Strong on one creator but weak on another? targeted cross-training.

TOOLS
Academy · Playbook · Session review · Per-skill score
2

Targeted replacement

For those who are chronically below threshold and haven't shown improvement. Action Center identifies candidates, suggests the best replacements (based on the creators the outgoing chatter works on), and prepares the list for HR.

TOOLS
Action Center · Swap suggestions · CSV export for HR

The two levers are complementary: every month, part of the team improves through training, part rotates through replacements. Together they push the average score upward.

04 — HOW WE MEASURE
The method · The formula

The score, explained
with an example.

"Is Marco a good chatter?"
Sounds easy, but it isn't. Marco works on two creators. On Sara he sells a lot, on Giulia very little. Maybe Giulia is a difficult creator. Comparing Marco with a colleague who works elsewhere would be unfair.

The solution: two combined rankings

vs Creator

Are you among the best
on the same creator?

Percentile vs other operators working on that creator.

vs Agency

At agency scale,
how good are you in absolute terms?

Percentile vs all HOC chatters for the month.

percentile vs Creator × 70% + percentile vs Agency × 30%
↓ then composed with consistency ↓
sales/shift × 85% + consistency × 15%
= score 0–100

The 70% rewards local merit (you're good where you work). The 30% anchors absolute performance (you can't end up Elite just because you're the least bad in a weak team). Min 3 shifts on a creator to be ranked.

The 6 tiers (percentile-based)

ELITE
top 10% · excellence
STRONG
top 25% · solid
GOOD
top 50% · reliable
AVERAGE
top 75% · in line
WEAK
top 90% · below average
CRITICAL
bottom 10% · unsustainable
05 — VALIDATION
Sales CP ↔ Infloww comparison

Two scores side-by-side,
discrepancies matter.

The Sales CP score (real sales) is the reference for decisions. The Infloww score (chat efficiency) stays visible as a second signal. When the two diverge by more than 10 points, a ↑/↓ arrow flags the interesting case — often this is where we understand if training is needed (Lever 1) or a replacement (Lever 2).

⚠️ Data quality note
On CreatorsPro some teams split the purch (the takes), so at the individual chatter level the data may be partially attributed to a teammate. On Infloww the data is per-chatter instead and therefore cleaner on the individual chat efficiency side. For this reason the comparison should be read with this nuance: discrepancies on teams that split purch are normal and not always a sign of a problem.

The 3 patterns the comparison reveals

🟢 CP Sales high + 🔵 Infloww low (e.g. Ismael, +34) → Ambiguous case, needs investigation
Two possible readings:
(a) Hyper-efficient — sells a lot with low chat volume. Sends few messages but closes well. Should be recognized as a real top performer.
(b) On strong creators that sell themselves — sales come regardless of the chat. In this case it's the creator's merit, not the chatter's.
To distinguish: look at the number of messages/shift and unique fans contacted. Operator drill-down reveals which of the two it is.
🟢 CP Sales low + 🔵 Infloww high → Lever 1 (TRAINING)
Good at chatting but converts little to $. Coaching opportunity on closing / PPV strategy. These often become top performers after 2-3 targeted sessions.
🟢 CP Sales ≈ Infloww → Linear decision
The two scores agree. More solid HR decision: the tops are really tops, the criticals are really criticals. Go straight to Lever 1 (recognize) or Lever 2 (replace).

The replacement matching algorithm

For each operator to be replaced, the system searches among Good+ for those who have already performed well exactly on the outgoing chatter's creators, weighting:

  • X's mix (50%) — the creators where X spent their time
  • Agency importance (50%) — the strategic creators for total revenue
Example: Livio Botolin (Critical)
Livio makes 60% of his sales on Eva Rizzoli and 40% on Celine Vitale. The system also weighs how much Eva contributes to agency revenue and suggests Tinx (fit 86.6 — has worked well precisely on Eva), NOT a generic Good performer working elsewhere. The suggested replacement maintains coverage on the right creators.

The output: CSV for HR

When you've marked N operators as "ready", the Export HR CSV button produces a file with: name, group, score, tier, sales, shifts, top creator, chosen replacement, date, notes. Ready for the replacement process.

07 — FLOW
Who does what, when

The monthly HR cycle

Four recurring steps, total ~30 minutes per month to close the cycle. From here both training (Lever 1) and replacements (Lever 2) start.

Step 1 · Auto

CP Sync

Once a month, click on "Sync CP". Imports wage records + exact takes from CreatorsPro via API.

Step 2 · Manual

Upload Infloww

Export "By time and employee" CSV from Infloww, upload to the webapp. ~2 minutes.

Step 3 · Review

Action Center

Open the panel, for each candidate click on the suggested replacement + HR button. ~15 min.

Step 4 · HR

CSV → HR

Click Export CSV, send to HR team. Replacement process starts.

What changes for each role

SM / Team Lead

Team performance view

Open the monthly leaderboard for their team. Compare CP with Infloww to identify coaching opportunities (Lever 1) before they become Lever 2 candidates.

HR

Replacement cycle

Action Center → replacement selection → CSV. The HR team takes the file and starts the exit interview / hiring replacement process.

Board

Aggregated view

Central hub with average agency score, monthly trend, active operators, creators covered. For strategic decisions on individual operators or creators, drill-down for details.

Methodology validation

②

HR cycle frequency

Proposal: monthly cycle (Action Center → CSV → HR → replacements within 2 weeks). Confirm or alternative?

③

Handover with HR

Can I coordinate directly with the HR team on the CSV handover and replacement process, or is collective discussion needed first?
